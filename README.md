# Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) in Morocco and Africa

An empirical research project exploring the macroeconomic and financial determinants of FDI across 17 African economies (2000–2022). This study was developed for the **International Finance** module at **ENCG Settat (2026)**.

## 📊 Project Overview
This repository contains the full analysis and findings regarding the dual role of Morocco as both a strategic recipient of global FDI and a major regional investor in Sub-Saharan Africa. The research bridges economic theory with real-world financial data using a panel of 391 observations.

## 🔍 Key Findings
* **Market Size & Solvency**: Analysis shows that per capita income and foreign exchange reserves are the primary drivers for attracting investment to the continent.
* **The Institutional Paradox**: The study highlights a negative correlation between governance quality and FDI in several African nations, often linked to heavy investment in extractive industries (oil/minerals).
* **Morocco's Performance**: 
    * Maintains an average inflation rate of **1.7%** (vs. 14.1% African average).
    * Financial development stands at **81% of GDP**, nearly triple the continental average.
    * Strong "push factors" driving Moroccan national champions (OCP, Attijariwafa, etc.) into regional markets.

## 🛠 Methodology
* **Scope**: 17 African countries over 23 years.
* **Data Sources**: World Bank (WDI & WGI), UNCTAD, and IMF.
* **Econometric Models**:
    * **Fixed Effects (FE)**: Used for inward FDI to control for country-specific variables.
    * **Ordinary Least Squares (OLS)**: Applied to time-series data for Moroccan outward investment.

## 📂 Report Structure
1. **Theoretical Foundations**: Institutional definitions, Greenfield vs. M&A, and Dunning’s OLI paradigm.
2. **Empirical Analysis**: Methodology, construction of the financial panel, and econometric results.
3. **Morocco Case Study**: Detailed profile of Morocco’s strategy as a regional investment hub.
